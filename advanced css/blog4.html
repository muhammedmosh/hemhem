NPFL: KWARA UNITED ISSUES APOLOGY TO KWARANS FOLLOWING TORNADOES' HOME DEFEAT

September 8, 2024 19:17

Kwara United Football Club extends its sincere apologies to its ardent fans and supporters in the wake of a disappointing 2-1 loss to Niger Tornadoes in their inaugural match of the Nigeria Professional Football League (NPFL) season.

Despite an auspicious start to the game with a strong display and commanding possession, Kwara United succumbed to goals from Joseph Godstime in the 36th minute and Sebiu Ibrahim in the 62nd minute.

Although Sadeeq Yusuf managed to salvage a goal in the dying moments of the match, it proved insufficient to salvage a draw for the home side.

Head coach Tunde Sanni attributed the defeat to his players' lack of poise and inefficiency in front of goal. He reassured fans that the team would diligently address these shortcomings and exhibit improvement in forthcoming matches.

Majin Mohammed, the coach of Niger Tornadoes, commended Kwara United's performance but emphasized the importance of unwavering focus throughout their fixtures.

Bashir Badawiy, the general manager of Kwara United, expressed remorse over the setback and tendered a heartfelt apology to the club's loyal supporters. He pledged that the technical staff would meticulously address the areas of weakness and strive to reclaim their winning form.
